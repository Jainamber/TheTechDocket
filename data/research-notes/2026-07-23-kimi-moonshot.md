## 1. Kimi Work

- Kimi Work is a desktop AI agent app for macOS and Windows, described by Moonshot as a "Local Agent designed for deep workflows" that mounts local folders, automates a browser via a "Kimi WebBridge" extension, and can run up to **300 parallel sub-agents** ("agent swarm") on a task. — SOURCE: https://www.kimi.com/resources/kimi-work-introduction
- It offers a "Goal Mode" for continuous autonomous task execution, scheduled/recurring background jobs (daily/weekly/monthly/once), and multi-format output generation (PowerPoint, Excel, PDF, Word, websites), plus "native connections to financial databases, World Bank data, and academic journals" including pre-integrated market data for A-shares, Hong Kong stocks, and US equities. — SOURCE: https://www.kimi.com/resources/kimi-work-introduction and https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/12/moonshot-ai-launches-kimi-work-a-local-desktop-agent-reportedly-running-on-kimi-k2-6-with-a-300-sub-agent-agent-swarm/
- **Launch date: mid-June 2026, NOT this week.** MarkTechPost's launch-day coverage is dated June 12, 2026 ("Moonshot AI Launches Kimi Work..."); a separate outlet dates a "globally" framed launch to June 24, 2026. This directly contradicts a "launched today" framing for July 23, 2026. — SOURCE: https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/12/moonshot-ai-launches-kimi-work-a-local-desktop-agent-reportedly-running-on-kimi-k2-6-with-a-300-sub-agent-agent-swarm/ and https://business20channel.tv/moonshot-ai-launches-kimi-work-desktop-agent-globally-in-2026-24-06-2026
- **Underlying model:** not officially confirmed by Moonshot on its own product/intro pages. "Independent community mentions" cited by MarkTechPost say it runs on **Kimi K2.6** (an open-weight MoE model Moonshot released April 20, 2026), not on the newer K3. — SOURCE: https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/12/moonshot-ai-launches-kimi-work-a-local-desktop-agent-reportedly-running-on-kimi-k2-6-with-a-300-sub-agent-agent-swarm/
- **Pricing:** Free to download; free-tier users get "limited free credits for initial trials" and can set up to 2 scheduled tasks; "more advanced paid plans are available" for further use, but exact tier pricing was not disclosed on the pages checked (a pricing page is linked but its content wasn't captured). — SOURCE: https://www.kimi.com/resources/kimi-work-introduction and https://www.kimi.com/products/kimi-work
- **Open-weight or proprietary:** Kimi Work the *application* is distributed as a proprietary, closed desktop app/managed service (not something users can self-host or modify) — the open-weight status applies to the underlying Kimi model family, not the Work app itself. — SOURCE: https://www.kimi.com/products/kimi-work
- **Availability outside China:** Not explicitly stated as region-restricted on Moonshot's own pages; Moonshot is described as "Beijing-based" and Kimi Work ships with pre-integrated US equities data, implying a global/US-facing rollout, but no explicit country list was found. UNVERIFIED as to any specific country restrictions. — SOURCE: https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/12/moonshot-ai-launches-kimi-work-a-local-desktop-agent-reportedly-running-on-kimi-k2-6-with-a-300-sub-agent-agent-swarm/
- HN commenters on the live "Kimi Work" thread raised privacy concerns (claiming the app has "unfettered read access" to local files despite messaging that authorization is required before modifying files), alleged the UI closely resembles OpenAI Codex and Claude Cowork (down to specific hero-image copy), and raised data-sovereignty concerns about sending data to a Chinese company. — SOURCE: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48981703

## 2. Kimi K3 — launch, specs, benchmarks, license, pricing

- **Two-part rollout, which is likely the source of date confusion:** K3 went live as an API/hosted service on **July 16, 2026** (via kimi.com, mobile apps, and the Kimi API Platform); full downloadable open weights are separately promised "by July 27, 2026." Some outlets round this to July 15, 17, or 18 depending on time zone/what stage they're describing. — SOURCE: https://emergent.sh/news/kimi-k3-release-date and https://www.kimi.com/blog/kimi-k3 and https://fortune.com/2026/07/16/moonshots-kimi-k3-pushes-chinese-ai-into-fable-level-territory/
- **Size/architecture:** 2.8 trillion total parameters, built on "Kimi Delta Attention and Attention Residuals," native vision capability, 1,048,576-token (1M) context window, and a "Stable LatentMoE" architecture that activates only 16 of 896 experts per token. Moonshot's own blog calls it "the world's first open 3T-class model." — SOURCE: https://www.kimi.com/blog/kimi-k3
- **Open weights / license:** Weights are not yet public as of this research (promised by July 27, 2026); once released, K3 is expected to ship under a **"Modified MIT" license** carrying an attribution clause that only triggers for products above 100 million monthly active users — the same structure Moonshot used for Kimi K2 in 2025. Until then it is API-access-only, i.e. effectively closed. — SOURCE: https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/18/kimi-k3-vs-deepseek-v4-pro-vs-glm-5-2-open-trillion-scale-moe-models-compared-on-benchmarks-license-and-serving-cost/
- **Pricing (API):** $0.30/million tokens (cache-hit input), $3/million tokens (cache-miss input), $15/million tokens (output). Average real-world cost per task cited at ~$0.94. — SOURCE: https://www.kimi.com/blog/kimi-k3 and https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/kimi-k3-achieves-3-in-the-artificial-analysis-intelligence-index-comparable-to-opus-4-8-and-gpt-5-5
- **Benchmark claims (Moonshot's own framing):** K3 "mostly beat[s] Claude Opus 4.8 max and GPT-5.5 high, while losing out to Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol." Moonshot's blog also states performance "still trails ... Claude Fable 5 and GPT 5.6 Sol" and that K3 "exhibits a noticeable gap in user experience compared with Claude Fable 5 and GPT 5.6 Sol." — SOURCE: https://www.kimi.com/blog/kimi-k3 and https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/kimi-k3/
- **Independent benchmark (Artificial Analysis):** K3 scores **57** on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, ranking **#3** overall, "comparable to Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5" but behind Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol. On agentic tasks (GDPval-AA v2) K3 reaches an Elo of 1668 (vs. 1190 for predecessor K2.6, 1494 for GPT-5.5, 1600 for Opus 4.8, 1760 for Fable 5); on knowledge work (AA-Briefcase) K3 reaches Elo 1547, "second only to Fable 5." K3 also uses "21% fewer output tokens" than K2.6 for higher scores. — SOURCE: https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/kimi-k3-achieves-3-in-the-artificial-analysis-intelligence-index-comparable-to-opus-4-8-and-gpt-5-5
- On Hacker News, a thread titled "Kimi K3 is now #1 in the Front end Code Arena with 1679 pts, surpassing Fable 5" (note: "1679 pts" refers to the Arena leaderboard Elo score, not Hacker News upvotes) itself received 102 HN points / 7 comments — this is likely the source of the "large HN point number" conflation in this week's coverage rather than a genuine 561-point thread. — SOURCE: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48939019
- **Cost comparison vs. Chinese rivals:** K3's $15/M output token price is far above DeepSeek V4 Pro (~$0.87/M, ~$0.04/task) and z.ai's GLM-5.2 (~$4.40/M, ~$0.32/task), and above K2.6's own $0.95/$4 pricing, though still roughly half the cost of Claude Opus 4.8 per task. — SOURCE: https://leanware.co/insights/kimi-k2 and https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/18/kimi-k3-vs-deepseek-v4-pro-vs-glm-5-2-open-trillion-scale-moe-models-compared-on-benchmarks-license-and-serving-cost/
- Existing enterprise/adoption anecdotes cited in coverage: Cursor reportedly used a Kimi model for its "Composer 2" coding agent; DoorDash reportedly delegated tasks to K2.6; Thinking Machines reportedly used K2.5 for training data (these are prior-model adoption claims, cited as market context for K3, not K3-specific). — SOURCE: https://fortune.com/2026/07/16/moonshots-kimi-k3-pushes-chinese-ai-into-fable-level-territory/

## 4. The distillation allegation and sanctions threat

### Timeline of allegations (there are TWO distinct episodes — do not conflate them)

- **Episode 1 — Anthropic's own accusation, February 24, 2026:** Anthropic publicly accused DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax of "industrial-scale distillation attacks on our models." Anthropic said it identified roughly 24,000 fraudulent accounts used to conduct "over 16 million exchanges" with Claude to extract model capabilities via API queries, and said MiniMax's distillation operation was an order of magnitude larger than Moonshot's, which was itself larger than DeepSeek's. Anthropic was reportedly concerned about "capabilities transfer" and "safeguards removal" enabling downstream military/intelligence use. OpenAI separately complained to Congress about DeepSeek using "new, obfuscated methods" for continued distillation. None of the three accused companies had responded as of that reporting. — SOURCE: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/24/anthropic-openai-china-firms-distillation-deepseek.html and https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-anthropic-accuses-deepseek
- **Episode 2 — US government (White House/Treasury) escalation, July 21-22, 2026:** This is the specific TechCrunch story in the research brief. Treasury Secretary **Scott Bessent** first raised the threat on Fox Business on **Tuesday, July 21, 2026**: "If we see, especially, that overseas models are stealing from our great companies, we have the ability to sanction them." He said the administration would "examine" Chinese open-source models for IP theft before acting. — SOURCE: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/21/us-threatens-sanctions-against-chinese-ai-models-over-ip-theft/
- The following day, **July 22, 2026**, **Michael Kratsios**, Director of the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy, made the specific Moonshot/Fable allegation (via social media post, per reporting — no documentary evidence was published alongside the claim): **"We have information that Moonshot AI distilled Anthropic's Fable for the development of its K3 model."** He further alleged Moonshot "developed a sophisticated internal platform to conduct large scale distillation against U.S. models, allowing them to quickly switch between multiple methods of access to avoid detection," and separately alleged Moonshot "acquired GB300-equipped servers and has accessed GB300s in Thailand, likely to train its AI models" — a reference to Nvidia GB300 chips that are barred from sale to Chinese companies under export controls. — SOURCE: https://americanbazaaronline.com/2026/07/22/us-accuses-chinas-moonshot-ai-of-distilling-anthropic-model-485043/ and https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/22/treasury-threatens-sanctions-after-white-house-claims-moonshot-distilled-anthropics-fable/
- Bessent reiterated/doubled down the same day (July 22): **"When [Chinese] firms conduct covert, industrial-scale distillation attacks that cross the line into IP theft, sanctions and Entity List designations will be on the table."** — SOURCE: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/22/treasury-threatens-sanctions-after-white-house-claims-moonshot-distilled-anthropics-fable/

### What "distillation" means here
- TechCrunch's own framing: distillation is "a common AI training technique in which a smaller model learns from the outputs of a larger one." It is not inherently illegal — the article notes it "can infringe intellectual property rights" but "is also used legitimately for optimization." The government's allegation is that Moonshot's use crossed from legitimate technique into large-scale, evasive IP theft (e.g., using fraudulent accounts/rotating access methods to harvest a rival's outputs at scale, per the February Anthropic claim). — SOURCE: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/22/treasury-threatens-sanctions-after-white-house-claims-moonshot-distilled-anthropics-fable/

### Responses
- **Anthropic:** No on-the-record Anthropic statement specifically about the July 22 White House/Kratsios claim was found in any source checked. UNVERIFIED / NOT FOUND for this episode. (Anthropic's own separate February 2026 accusation is documented above in Episode 1.)
- **Moonshot AI:** No response found. TechCrunch explicitly states it "reached out to Moonshot and the Treasury for comment" but had not received replies at publication. A separate outlet similarly states "it was not immediately clear whether Moonshot AI had responded to the claims." — SOURCE: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/22/treasury-threatens-sanctions-after-white-house-claims-moonshot-distilled-anthropics-fable/ and https://americanbazaaronline.com/2026/07/22/us-accuses-chinas-moonshot-ai-of-distilling-anthropic-model-485043/
- A separate piece (not independently verified against a primary technical source) claims a third-party "statistical analysis" purports to show Kimi K3 was distilled from Fable — flagged here as a headline only; UNVERIFIED, methodology not reviewed. — SOURCE: https://glitchwire.com/news/new-statistical-analysis-suggests-kimi-k3-was-distilled-from-anthropics-fable-ad/

### Sanctions: threatened, not imposed
- As of this research (July 23, 2026), **no sanctions have been imposed** — Bessent's and Kratsios's statements are explicitly framed as warnings/threats ("will be on the table," "have the ability to sanction them"), contingent on further investigation. — SOURCE: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/21/us-threatens-sanctions-against-chinese-ai-models-over-ip-theft/
- Broader related-but-distinct policy context found in the same news cycle: reports that the NSA and the White House Office of the National Cyber Director have been pushing for tougher measures — including potentially adding Chinese AI labs to the Commerce Department's "Entity List" (which "stops firms from using the tech without government permission") and even "a total ban on foreign open-weights systems" for US users. This is reported as separate from, but related to, the distillation/sanctions threat, and is also not yet enacted. — SOURCE: https://www.neowin.net/news/uh-oh-the-trump-admin-doesnt-like-chinas-kimi-k3-ai-model-and-could-ban-it-for-americans/

## 6. Timeline context — Kimi K2's prior reception

- Kimi K2 launched **July 2025** under a **Modified MIT License** (permitting commercial/research use, with an attribution requirement only above 100 million monthly users or $20 million monthly revenue — the same license structure carried into K3). Reported benchmark claims at the time: 97.4% on MATH-500, 69.6 average on AIME 2024, 65.8% on SWE-bench Verified, 53.7% on LiveCodeBench v6, and 65.8 on Tau2 Telecom (vs. 38.6 for GPT-4.1); the model supported 200-300 sequential autonomous tool calls without human intervention. — SOURCE: https://leanware.co/insights/kimi-k2
- Reception was described as mixed: praise for open-sourcing trillion-parameter-class capability, strong agentic-workflow performance, and cost efficiency, versus criticism over content-filtering restrictions, integration friction, and perceptions that benchmark claims were "benchmaxed" rather than reflective of everyday usability. — SOURCE: https://leanware.co/insights/kimi-k2
- AI researcher/commentator **Nathan Lambert** was quoted describing a later K2 variant ("K2 Thinking") as representing "the closest open models have been to the closed frontier of performance ever." — SOURCE: https://leanware.co/insights/kimi-k2 (originally from Nathan Lambert's Interconnects newsletter, not independently re-fetched in this pass)
