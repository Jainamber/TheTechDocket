## Claim 6: Benchmarks / capability claims

Two claims surfaced that are in tension with each other; both are reported here rather than resolved, since full article bodies could not be fetched.

1. officechai headline: "Gemini 3.6 Flash Beats Gemini 3.5 Flash, Gemini 3.1 Pro On Most Benchmarks" — https://officechai.com/ai/gemini-3-6-flash-benchmarks/
2. Artificial Analysis (independent, well-regarded model-benchmarking firm) via its official X account: "Google has released Gemini 3.6 Flash and Gemini 3.5 Flash-Lite. Both halve time per task relative to their predecessors and increase token efficiency, Gemini 3.5 Flash-Lite improves by 11 Intelligence Index points while Gemini 3.6 Flash does not improve in intelligence over 3.5" — https://x.com/ArtificialAnlys/status/2079596244339707956

These two claims are not strictly contradictory (a model can win "most" individual benchmarks while an aggregate "Intelligence Index" doesn't move, or the two sources may be measuring different things), but an editor should note: independent-benchmark data (Artificial Analysis) frames Gemini 3.6 Flash primarily as a **speed/efficiency** upgrade (halved time-per-task) rather than a **capability/intelligence** upgrade, while Gemini 3.5 Flash-Lite is described as the one with a measured intelligence gain (+11 points on Artificial Analysis's index).

Separately, GitHub's own X account (launch/integration partner, semi-official) stated: "In testing, it demonstrated higher task-completion rates and better token efficiency than Gemini 3.5 Flash" (coding-agent context specifically) — https://x.com/github/status/2079588576358998350

**VERIFICATION STATUS:**
- "Beats on most benchmarks" (officechai): **(c)** single-source.
- "Halves time-per-task, no intelligence-index gain over 3.5" (Artificial Analysis): **(c)** single-source, but from an independent, reputable benchmarking organization — carries more evidentiary weight than a typical single blog, but is still only one outlet's data in the results gathered.
- Exact context-window size (tokens) for Gemini 3.6 Flash: **(d)** — not found/confirmed. (One unrelated hit for a different, earlier model — "Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite: 1M Context, 363 Tokens/Sec" — was excluded here since it describes a different model, not 3.6 Flash or 3.5 Flash-Lite; do not conflate.)
